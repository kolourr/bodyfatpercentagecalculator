A.I. BODY FAT PERCENTAGE CALCULATOR

Our Body Fat Percentage Calculator can be used to estimate your total body fat percentage from a single image of yourself.

In the image, you must stand straight, flex, keep your hands to the side and be the only one in it. Men must be shirtless and women in gym attire/form fitting clothing. Use this example as a reference.

When you upload your picture, a neural network (A.I.) gives you an answer within 5 seconds (based on the closest body fat percentage range). In essence, we help you visually estimate your body fat percentage.

In addition to your body fat percentage, we provide you with:

1. Body fat percentage pictures of men and women (with analysis).

2. Breakdown of visceral and subcutaneous fat and android and gynoid distribution.

3. How hormones like insulin, leptin, ghrelin and cortisol influence body fat accumulation.

4. Information on how you can lose weight fast without exercise.

Whose Idea Was This?

That would be Bruce Rebello, an industrial engineer and amateur programmer from Toronto, Canada.

My journey with trying to lose fat and study programming started around the same time in November of 2018. These two areas of my life would converge during a subway ride in early May 2019 while listening to The Power of Now by Eckhart Tolle. Wild, right? Let me explain.



2018 wasn’t the greatest year for me, professionally or personally. My life in an alternate universe was probably sour as well. And to top it off, I was getting fat. Really fat.

I knew that something had to be done about my health, and also maybe it was time to pick up a new skill to bring the drive back into my life. So I took a course in Python programming and dove into books on nutrition. As it turned out, I really enjoyed learning to program and was also drawn to the work of Dr. Jason Fung on fat reduction.

Reading The Obesity Code: Unlocking the Secrets of Weight Loss helped bust many myths and lies on nutrition and working out that had consumed me for most of my adult life. I highly recommend the book to anyone stuck in a never ending cycle of going on diets only to gain all the weight back plus some.

This was the only book that addressed the root cause of obesity and how it could be reversed permanently. As part of making this positive change in my life, I got a Dexa Scan. As I mentioned in the sections above, Dexa Scans are by far the most accurate forms of fat detection, except they cost $150/session.

I started 2019 with a renewed sense of self and a focus on eating healthy and mastering programming. By mid April 2019, I lost quite a bit of fat and had completed my initial course in Python. With the same forward momentum, I decided to jump into Fast AI’s course on Deep Learning (A.I. Development).

The course is free and if you are willing to do the work, you will learn a lot. One of the homework assignments was to build an image classification model on something that interested you. I didn’t think much about it at the time and figured I would build an image classifier on differentiating between species of birds or wild animals.

I was listening to the audiobook of The Power of Now by Eckhart Tolle on the subway ride to my day job when the idea for this app hit me. For those who are interested, the focus of Tolle’s book is on staying present (in the moment), training your mind to reach your deeper self, on creating a blank canvas in the mind for ideas, and learning to fight off pain that stems from your mind.

What if I build a dataset that referenced Dexa Scan Images representing different body fat percentages of men and women, train a neural net on it, and use that to help anyone, anywhere in the world get their body fat percentage with just a single picture. Now, the body fat percentages obtained through the app could never match the accuracy of a Dexa Scan. However, it would certainly give you a pretty good idea of where you stand.

This could go a long way in keeping your health in check and would also save you $150 every time you want to figure out what your body fat percentage is. It was a long shot that it would work, but as this was something I wished I had had when I started thinking about losing fat, I saw great value in it.

I do hope you find it useful and keep coming back to check on your progress. If you want to reach out, send an email to contact@estimatebodyfat.com
